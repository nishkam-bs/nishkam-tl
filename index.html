Ethos

Teaching & Learning at Nishkam School

At Nishkam, the classroom is not only a place of academic achievement. It is where virtues are lived, identities are shaped, and every child is truly seen. Our approach to teaching and learning reflects who we are as a community.

Pillar I
Faith-Inspired

Drawing on the wisdom of all traditions. The teacher who approaches their craft with humility and openness creates the conditions for every child to flourish.

Pillar II
Virtues-Led

Peacefulness, diligence and determination — practised in ourselves first. Consistent, caring classrooms are an act of professional virtue, not technique alone.

Pillar III
Aspiring for Excellence

Becoming the best teachers we can possibly be. Rehearsing our craft, seeking honest feedback, and never settling for less than every pupil deserves.

"To empower children with virtues that enable them to excel academically and spiritually, inspiring them to serve humanity selflessly — with an abundance of love, compassion and forgiveness."

Virtues & Golden Threads

The threads that run through everything.

The Golden Threads are the spiritual and moral foundations of life at Nishkam School. They run through every lesson, every interaction, every act of teaching and learning.

Golden Thread 1
love and forgivenessvsenmity and hate
Virtue
love

Love is caring for someone, wanting to be near them and wanting to share with them. It is a special feeling that fills your heart. Loving people is treating them with special care and kindness. Love is treating other people just as you would like them to treat you — with care and respect.

I express love for the Creator by caring for others and creation.
Golden Thread 2
peace & collaborationvsblame & conflict
Virtue of the Week
peacefulness

Peacefulness is an inner sense of calm that can come in moments of silent gratitude or prayer. It is facing your fears and then letting them go. Peacefulness is giving up the love of power for the power of love. Peace in the world begins with peace in your heart.

I use peaceful language even when I am angry. I appreciate differences. Peacefulness helps me to be calm.
Golden Thread 3
Trust in God
Virtue
trust

Trust is having faith, hope and a positive outlook. It is relying on and believing in someone or something — having confidence that the right thing will come about, without trying to control it. Trust is being sure, deep down, that there is some gift or learning in everything life brings. We know we are never alone.

I believe there is some lesson in everything that happens. I let trust take away my fears.
Our Classroom Virtues

Compassion · Humility · Determination

🤲
Compassion
In Teaching & Learning
Consider the impact of every decision on every child. When a teacher adapts their approach for a struggling pupil — stays late, rephrases, notices — they are choosing compassion over convenience.
🙏
Humility
In Teaching & Learning
True humility is not thinking less of yourself — it is thinking of yourself less. The best teachers listen more than they speak, acknowledge what they do not yet know, and remain open to learning. Man neeva, mat uchi.
🏹
Determination
In Teaching & Learning
Persevere through every challenging class, every difficult week. Treat every lesson that does not go to plan as a stepping stone. The most impactful teachers are the most determined to keep improving.
