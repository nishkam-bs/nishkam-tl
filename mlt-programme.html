Middle Leadership Development

Empowering leaders to influence culture, drive improvement, and lead with clarity and care.

Current & aspiring middle leaders
6 sessions · 60 mins each
Evidence-based leadership principles
Sessions

Six sessions. Lead with clarity.

Each session includes a CPD presentation, facilitator-led activities, self-directed reflection, and practical frameworks to implement immediately.

01
What is a Middle Leader?
Exploring the unique role of middle leaders as the bridge between whole-school strategy and classroom practice. Defines the role and responsibilities of a middle leader, identifies the six key traits — credibility, clarity, consistency, communication, courage, and coaching mindset — and asks leaders to reflect on how their leadership shapes culture and outcomes.
Define the role and responsibilities of a middle leader
Identify key traits: the Six Cs
Reflect on how leadership shapes culture and outcomes

Leading from the Middle
Translating whole-school vision into action with clarity, consistency, and compassion. Explores the six core behaviours of effective middle leadership, develops strategies for visibility, accountability, and coaching — and reflects on how consistency in small things creates momentum and stability across a team.
Understand the six core behaviours of effective middle leadership

03
Leading When It Is Hard
Mastering courageous conversations and constructive feedback. Teaches leaders to shift from teacher lens to leader lens under pressure, practise strategies for difficult conversations and feedback that lands without damaging trust, and build confidence in holding people to account with both clarity and care.
Shift from teacher lens to leader lens under pressure
Practise strategies for difficult conversations and feedback
Build confidence in accountability with clarity and care

04
Developing Others
Shifting from managing to growing people. Introduces the developer lens vs manager lens — the most powerful thing a leader can do is build capacity in others. Explores evidence-based strategies for coaching and adult learning, and practises creating stretch opportunities that empower staff rather than overwhelm them.
Understand the developer lens vs manager lens
Explore research-based strategies for coaching and adult learning
Practise creating stretch opportunities that empower staff
CoachingDeveloper LensAdult LearningStretch

05
Staff Morale
Why morale matters and how leadership behaviours directly influence team wellbeing. Draws on Maslow, Herzberg, and transformational leadership to help leaders identify factors that boost or drain morale, and practises coaching conversations that support staff wellbeing in practical, everyday ways.
Understand key theories: Maslow, Herzberg, transformational leadership
Identify factors that boost or drain morale in your team
Practise coaching conversations to support staff wellbeing
WellbeingMotivationHerzbergCulture

06
Reflection & Next Steps
Consolidating learning, celebrating progress, and setting longer-term goals. Leaders reflect on programme impact, identify strengths, challenges, and concrete next steps, and are encouraged to begin a research or inquiry project to deepen and sustain their development beyond the programme.
Reflect on the impact of the programme on your leadership
Identify strengths, challenges, and next steps
Set long-term goals and consider a research inquiry project
